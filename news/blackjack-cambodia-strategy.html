Blackjack Rules – Get Closer to 21 Than the Dealer

Blackjack (also called 21) is a card game where the objective is to get a hand total closer to 21 than the dealer without going over. Number cards count at face value, face cards count as 10, and aces count as 1 or 11. A blackjack (ace + 10-value card) pays 3:2. You can hit (take another card), stand (keep your hand), double down (double your bet for one more card), or split (split a pair into two hands). The dealer must hit on 16 or less and stand on 17 or more in most variants.

Cambodian players can learn blackjack quickly. The rules are straightforward, and basic strategy charts are widely available to guide optimal play.

Basic Strategy – Reduce the House Edge

Basic strategy is a set of optimal decisions for every possible hand combination based on the dealer's upcard. It is derived from mathematical analysis and can reduce the house edge to around 0.5% or less. For example: always hit on 16 or less when the dealer shows 7 or higher; always stand on 17 or more; double down on 11 when the dealer shows 2–10; split aces and 8s. Strategy charts are available online—print one or keep it on your phone until the decisions become second nature.

Basic strategy does not guarantee wins, but it maximises your chances over the long term. Avoid insurance bets—they have a high house edge.

Blackjack Side Bets – When to Avoid Them

Side bets like Perfect Pairs, 21+3, or Insurance add variety but have high house edges (often 5–25%). Insurance is offered when the dealer shows an ace—it pays 2:1 if the dealer has blackjack. Mathematically, insurance is a poor bet. Avoid side bets unless you are playing purely for entertainment. Basic strategy on the main bet gives you the best odds.
