What is Responsible Gambling?

Responsible gambling means treating gambling as paid entertainment. Set a budget before you play and never exceed it. Never chase losses—increasing bets to recover losses usually leads to bigger losses. Do not gamble when upset, stressed, or under the influence of alcohol. Gambling should be fun. If it stops being fun, take a break. Only gamble with money you can afford to lose. Never use money needed for rent, bills, or family.

Signs of Problem Gambling

Problem gambling can affect anyone. Signs include: gambling with money you cannot afford to lose, chasing losses, lying about gambling, neglecting work or family, feeling anxious or irritable when not gambling, and borrowing money to gamble. If you recognise these signs in yourself or someone you know, seek help. Gambling addiction is treatable.
