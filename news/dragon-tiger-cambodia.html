Dragon Tiger Rules – Simple Card Comparison

Dragon Tiger uses a standard deck. One card is dealt to Dragon and one to Tiger. The highest card wins. Ace is low (1), King is high. Suits determine the winner if cards are equal (Spades > Hearts > Clubs > Diamonds). You bet on Dragon, Tiger, or Tie. Dragon and Tiger pay 1:1 (or 0.9:1 with commission). Tie typically pays 8:1 or 11:1. Side bets on suit or whether the next card will be higher or lower are sometimes available. The game is similar to baccarat but even simpler—no drawing rules, just two cards compared.

Dragon Tiger Strategy – Bet Selection

Dragon Tiger is a game of chance. Each round is independent—past results do not affect future outcomes. The Dragon and Tiger bets have a similar house edge (around 3.7% with standard payouts). The best approach is to stick to Dragon or Tiger, set a budget, and enjoy the fast-paced action. Avoid the Tie bet unless you are playing for fun.
