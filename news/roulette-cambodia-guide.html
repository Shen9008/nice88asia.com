Roulette Basics – How the Wheel Works

Roulette is a game of chance where a ball is spun around a wheel with numbered pockets. You bet on where the ball will land—a specific number, a colour (red/black), odd/even, or a range of numbers. Payouts vary: a straight-up bet (single number) pays 35:1; red/black pays 1:1. The dealer spins the wheel and drops the ball. When the ball settles, winning bets are paid. European vs American Roulette

European roulette has 37 pockets (1–36 plus a single zero). The house edge is 2.7%. American roulette has 38 pockets (1–36 plus single and double zero). The extra zero increases the house edge to 5.3%. Always choose European roulette when available—it offers better odds. French roulette is similar to European but may include the La Partage rule (half your even-money bet returned if the ball lands on zero), further reducing the house edge.

Inside bets (single numbers) have higher payouts but lower probability. The Martingale (doubling after a loss) can lead to large losses—avoid it. Set a budget and stick to it. European roulette has the best odds (2.7% house edge). French roulette with La Partage can be even better. Avoid American roulette (5.3% house edge) when European is available.

What is the La Partage rule?

La Partage applies to even-money bets (red/black, odd/even) when the ball lands on zero. Instead of losing your full bet, you receive half back. This reduces the house edge on even-money bets.
