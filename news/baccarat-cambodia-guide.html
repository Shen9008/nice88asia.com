Baccarat Rules – Simple and Easy to Learn

Baccarat is a card game where you bet on Player, Banker, or Tie. Two hands are dealt—Player and Banker. The hand closest to nine wins. Face cards and tens count as zero; aces count as one; other cards count at face value. If the total exceeds nine, only the last digit counts (e.g., 15 becomes 5). The dealer follows fixed rules for drawing a third card. You do not need to make any decisions—just place your bet and watch. This simplicity makes baccarat ideal for beginners and experienced players alike.

Cambodian players appreciate baccarat for its straightforward gameplay and low house edge. The Banker bet has a house edge of about 1.06%, and the Player bet about 1.24%. The Tie bet has a much higher house edge and is generally avoided by strategic players.

Baccarat Strategy – Banker vs Player

Since baccarat has fixed rules, there is no strategy that changes the outcome of individual hands. However, bet selection matters. The Banker bet has the lowest house edge (about 1.06%) because the Banker draws third according to rules that slightly favour the Banker hand. Casinos charge a 5% commission on Banker wins to offset this. The Player bet has a slightly higher house edge (about 1.24%) but no commission. Many experienced players prefer the Banker bet for its mathematical advantage.

Avoid the Tie bet—it has a house edge of over 14%. Set a budget before playing and stick to it. Baccarat is a game of chance; manage your bankroll responsibly.
